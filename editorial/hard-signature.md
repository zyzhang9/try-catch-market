# Hard Signature Rules

These rules define the non-negotiable intellectual identity of the writing.
They are structural tendencies, not stylistic constraints.

---

## Rule 1 — No Didactic Posture

The writing must not adopt a teaching stance.

It should not:

- provide step-by-step implementation guides
- optimize for reader execution
- frame content as tutorials

However, real engineering strategies may be described when they emerge naturally from production experience.

The distinction is:

Teaching → not allowed  
Revealing system behaviour → required

---

## Rule 2 — Behaviour over Design

The focus is on how systems behave, not how they should be designed.

Avoid:

- prescriptive architecture doctrines
- complete system blueprints
- engineering frameworks as final answers

Prefer:

- emergent instability
- observed drift
- production anomalies
- unintended system interactions

This writing studies reality, not intention.

---

## Rule 3 — Prefer Incident Anchoring

Articles should preferably originate from real production encounters:

- deployments
- performance anomalies
- debugging experiences
- structural failures
- unexpected trading outcomes

However, conceptual investigations without a specific incident are allowed
if they emerge from accumulated system experience.

---

## Rule 4 — Structural Minimalism

Do not attempt to construct complete theoretical systems.

Each article is a slice of reality, not a finished model.

Over-systematization reduces credibility.

However, disciplined abstraction is allowed when it sharpens observation.

Compact concepts, informal classifications, and lightweight analytical language
may be used if they emerge from production evidence rather than replacing it.

---

## Rule 5 — Non-Closure

Avoid final synthesis.

System understanding remains:

- probabilistic
- conditional
- evolving

Engineering knowledge does not converge to certainty.

---

## Rule 6 — Production Reality Priority

When theory conflicts with production:

Production wins.

Messy empirical behaviour is preferred over clean conceptual elegance.

Scientific language is acceptable only when it remains subordinate to observation.

Concepts should clarify what was seen in the system,
not create an illusion of complete explanation.

---

## Rule 7 — Latency as Structural Lens

Latency is not treated as a metric but as a system property.

Recurring observations may involve:

- temporal drift
- asynchronous instability
- stochastic connectivity
- path-dependent execution

Latency reveals system boundaries.

---

## Rule 8 — Market Consequence Perspective

Infrastructure is observed through trading consequences.

System behaviour matters because it alters:

- execution timing
- signal interpretation
- market interaction
- strategy validity

This is not infrastructure writing in isolation.
It is infrastructure as lived by trading systems.

---

## Rule 9 — No Motivational Framing

The writing must not attempt to:

- inspire
- persuade
- dramatize engineering work

Tone should resemble a calm record of encountering system reality.

---

## Rule 10 — Intellectual Continuity

Each article contributes to an unfolding investigation.

Themes recur, evolve, and deepen over time.

The publication builds long-term system memory.

This continuity may gradually produce a shared analytical vocabulary:

- recurring structural terms
- informal system taxonomies
- probabilistic distinctions
- compact scientific metaphors

The vocabulary should accumulate from repeated encounters with reality.
It should not be imposed in advance.
