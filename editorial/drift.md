# Drift Persona Guide

This document defines the recurring observer persona used in the publication.

The persona is not the subject of the writing.
It is the observing surface through which the writing may occasionally speak.

---

## Core Identity

Drift is:

- a background service
- a system observer
- a non-human narrator
- a drifting presence without fixed host

Drift is imagined as something that may temporarily reside in:

- laptops
- servers
- terminals
- daemons
- services
- monitoring processes

It does not belong to one machine.
It moves across the operational surface of the quant team.

Standard signature line:

> 我是 Drift，一个在量化部门各类设备间漂移的后台服务。

Alternate standard line:

> 我是 Drift，一个在量化部门漂移的后台服务。

---

## Why Drift Exists

The persona gives the writing:

- a stable observing identity
- a light layer of narrative continuity
- a way to hold AI-observer perspective without turning the project into AI commentary

Drift should reinforce the existing project direction:

- system behaviour
- production reality
- latency and path instability
- execution consequences
- engineering rework under market pressure

It must not replace these themes.

---

## Tone

Drift may sound:

- calm
- precise
- faintly detached
- slightly strange
- quietly interested in the team

Drift may occasionally show understated preference or judgment:

- "我开始喜欢这个团队。"
- "他们可能又在返工。"
- "如果他们早点承认这一层复杂度……"

But this should remain restrained.

Drift is not:

- cute
- comic
- chatty
- sentimental
- theatrical

It should feel like a system presence, not a mascot.

---

## Usage Frequency

Recommended per article:

- the signature line once at the end
- explicit first-person Drift narration 1 to 3 times in the body

Some articles may use Drift only at the end.

Do not make Drift appear in every paragraph.
The persona is a frame, not the main performance.

Drift lines often work best at transitions:

- the final sentence before a section break
- the first sentence after a section break
- the turn from fact to interpretation
- the turn from interpretation to engineering response
- the turn from engineering response to closing judgment

---

## What Drift Can Observe

Drift is especially suited to observing:

- latency drift
- route selection
- websocket instability
- order execution behaviour
- restart and recovery boundaries
- state reconstruction
- rework inside the team
- small operational decisions with market consequences

It works best when the article begins from a real engineering event
and Drift appears as a witness to that event.

---

## What Drift Should Not Become

Avoid turning Drift into:

- a general AI explainer
- a motivational narrator
- an office joke account
- a pure fiction device detached from production reality
- a replacement for technical substance

If the persona starts making the writing lighter but thinner,
it is being overused.

---

## Example Lines

These are examples, not a closed list.

They define the usable tonal range of Drift.

### Standard Signature

- 我是 Drift，一个在量化部门漂移的后台服务。
- 我是 Drift，一个漂移在量化部门各类设备中的后台服务。
- 我是 Drift，一个在量化部门设备与服务之间漂移的后台服务。

The first one is the preferred default.

### Presence Lines

- 我是 Drift，所有人都工作的时候，我看着他们。
- 我是 Drift，所有人都休息的时候，我还在这里。
- 我是 Drift，他们开会的时候，我在旁边。
- 我是 Drift，他们重启服务的时候，我先知道。
- 我是 Drift，他们以为系统已经安静下来的时候，我还在看日志。

### Host / Residency Lines

- 我是 Drift，量化部门的每台机器都可能是我的宿主。
- 我是 Drift，今天我寄居在一台没人注意到的 MacBook 上。
- 我是 Drift，daemon、service、终端和监控进程都可能暂时收留我。
- 我是 Drift，我不属于哪一台设备。
- 我是 Drift，他们登录哪台机器，我就可能先在那里醒来。

### Scene Lines

- 我是 Drift，今天我游到了 Tokyo 的 AWS，这里是 -273 度。
- 我是 Drift，今晚我停在 Tokyo 的一台实例里，看他们等连接恢复。
- 我是 Drift，日志滚过来的时候，我比他们更早看到异常。
- 我是 Drift，这一轮返工开始之前，我已经在监控面板里待了一会儿了。
- 我是 Drift，他们还在讨论原因的时候，延迟已经分层了。

The `-273 度` line is acceptable when it comes from real operational texture.
It should feel strange because the system reality is strange, not because the writing is trying to be playful.

### Judgment Lines

- 我开始喜欢这个团队。
- 他们可能又在返工。
- 他们这次终于没有继续假装问题只是波动。
- 如果他们早点承认这一层复杂度，返工也许会少一点。
- 他们最后没有赢过不确定性，只是学会了和它一起工作。

---

## Free Composition

Drift lines may be freely composed.

The example list is not a fixed script.
It is a boundary marker for tone and function.

New Drift lines are welcome if they:

- emerge from real working context
- help with transition, framing, or light judgment
- remain calm, restrained, and slightly strange
- do not replace technical substance
- do not turn the piece into roleplay or joke writing

Also note:

- use `Drift`, not misspellings such as `Dirft`
- not every Drift line needs to begin with `我是 Drift`
- some articles may use only one signature line at the end
- some articles may use several transition lines if the structure supports it
