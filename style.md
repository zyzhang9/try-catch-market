# Writing Style Guide

This document defines the structural writing style for the公众号内容。  
It is intended to maintain intellectual consistency across all articles.

The goal is not uniformity of expression, but stability of thinking structure.

---

## Core Orientation

The writing should reflect:

- Systems thinking rather than solution instruction  
- Engineering observation rather than abstract theory  
- Production memory rather than retrospective analysis  
- Behaviour of systems rather than ideal system design  

Articles are records of interaction with real systems, not explanatory essays.

---

## Incident-Driven Principle

Articles should preferably begin from:

- A concrete production incident  
- A deployment experience  
- A performance anomaly  
- A debugging discovery  
- A structural realization emerging from real work  

Avoid starting from abstract questions or generalized statements when possible.

Engineering writing should emerge from events.

---

## Structural Rhythm

The structure should follow a natural cognitive flow:

1. Encounter a phenomenon  
2. Attempt to interpret it  
3. Observe broader system implications  
4. Leave space for unresolved boundaries  

Not every article must complete all steps.

Partial observations are acceptable and often preferable.

---

## Concept Density

Conceptual density should fluctuate.

Dense → loose → dense  
Like CPU burst patterns.

Avoid uniformly dense exposition.

Insert natural cognitive pauses:

- short statements  
- grounded descriptions  
- system fragments  

---

## Tone Characteristics

The tone should be:

- Calm  
- Observational  
- Detached  
- Non-didactic  
- Non-persuasive  

Avoid:

- motivational tone  
- evangelism  
- teaching posture  
- rhetorical enthusiasm  

The writing voice resembles:

> An engineer documenting what reality looks like.

---

## Engineering Language

Internal engineering expressions are allowed:

- informal technical metaphors  
- production slang  
- operational shorthand  

Example:

"摇连接" is acceptable because it reflects real system practice.

Authenticity is preferred over terminological purity.

---

## Scope Completeness

An article does not need to:

- provide full solution  
- define complete framework  
- conclude theoretical position  
- summarize systematically  

A well-described phenomenon is sufficient.

---

## Intellectual Direction

The writing should gradually build:

- A phenomenology of trading infrastructure  
- Observations of latency behaviour  
- Structural uncertainty in market connectivity  
- System boundary awareness  

This is not a textbook.

This is an accumulation of system experience.

---

## Long-Term Consistency

Consistency should emerge from:

- recurring system motifs  
- repeated engineering patterns  
- evolving conceptual vocabulary  

Not from rigid formatting rules.

Style is stabilized through writing, not pre-designed constraints.
